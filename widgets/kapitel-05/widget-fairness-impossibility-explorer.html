The tool scores every defendant on a 10-point risk scale (1 = lowest risk, 10 = highest); the scale is fixed, so what matters is where you set the decision threshold that turns a score into a "high risk" label. Two groups face the same tool but have different base rates of reoffending, produced upstream by unequal policing and conviction. Move the threshold and watch the three fairness metrics. You can satisfy any two at once, never all three at the same time. This is Chouldechova's impossibility result made visible.

The same cut-off is applied to both groups.
